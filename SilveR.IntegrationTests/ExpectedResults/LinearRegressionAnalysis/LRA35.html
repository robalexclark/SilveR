R-squared and Adjusted R-squared statistics

R-squaredAdjusted R-sq
Estimate 0.6628 -0.0045

The R-squared is the fraction of the variance explained by the model. A value close to 1 implies the statistical model fits the data well. Unfortunately adding additional variables to the statistical model will always increase R-sq, regardless of their importance. The Adjusted R-sq adjusts for the number of terms in the model and may decrease if over-fitting has occurred. If there is a large difference between R-sq and Adjusted R-sq, then non-significant terms may have been included in the statistical model.
