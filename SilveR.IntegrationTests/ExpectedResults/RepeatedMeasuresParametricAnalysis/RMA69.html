InVivoStat Repeated Measures Parametric Analysis

Response, covariate and covariance structure

The PVtestResp1 response is currently being analysed by the Repeated Measures Parametric Analysis module, with PVTestCOV1a fitted as a covariate.

The repeated measures mixed model analysis is using the compound symmetric covariance structure to model the within-subject correlations. When using this structure you are assuming sphericity and also that the variability of responses is the same at each level of PVTestDay1, see Pinherio and Bates (2002). These assumptions may not hold in practice.

Plot of the response vs. the covariate, categorised by the primary factor

Tip: In order to decide whether it is helpful to fit the covariate, the following should be considered:

a) Is there a relationship between the response and the covariate? (N.B., It is only worth fitting the covariate if there is a strong positive (or negative) relationship between them: i.e., the lines on the plot should not be horizontal).

b) Is the relationship similar for all treatments? (The lines on the plot should be approximately parallel).

c) Is the covariate influenced by the treatment? (We assume the covariate is not influenced by the treatment and so there should be no separation of the treatment groups along the x-axis on the plot).

These issues are discussed in more detail in Morris (1999).

Warning

Unfortunately one or more of the residual degrees of freedom in the above table are low (less than 5). This may make the estimation of the underlying variability, and hence the results of the statistical tests, unreliable. This can be caused by attempting to fit too many factors, and their interactions, in the statistical model. Where appropriate we recommend you fit some of the 'Treatment' factors as 'Other design' factors. This will remove their interactions from the statistical model and therefore increase the residual degrees of freedom.

Analysis description

The data were analysed using a 2-way repeated measures mixed model approach, with PVTestTreat1 as the treatment factor, PVTestDay1 as the repeated factor and PVTestCOV1a as the covariate.This was followed by Planned Comparisons on the predicted means to compare the levels of the effect PVTestTreat1 * PVTestDay1.

The compound symmetric covariance structure was used to model the within-subject correlations. When using this structure we assumed that the variability of the responses was the same at each level of PVTestDay1 and the correlation between responses from any pair of levels of PVTestDay1 is the same.

A full description of mixed model theory, including information on the R nlme package used by InVivoStat, can be found in Venables and Ripley (2003) and Pinherio and Bates (2002).

For more information on the theoretical approaches that are implemented within this module, see Bate and Clark (2014).
