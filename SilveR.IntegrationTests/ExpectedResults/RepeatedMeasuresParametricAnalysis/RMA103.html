InVivoStat Repeated Measures Parametric Analysis

Response, covariates and covariance structure

The Resp 1x response is currently being analysed by the Repeated Measures Parametric Analysis module, with Cov1, Cov6 and Cov7 fitted as covariates.

The repeated measures mixed model analysis is using the compound symmetric covariance structure to model the within-subject correlations. When using this structure you are assuming sphericity and also that the variability of responses is the same at each level of Day 1x, see Pinherio and Bates (2002). These assumptions may not hold in practice.

The analysis implements the Kenward-Roger approximation of the degress of freedom, Kenward and Roger (1997).

Tip: In order to decide whether it is helpful to fit the covariate, the following should be considered:

a) Is there a relationship between the response and the covariate? (N.B., It is only worth fitting the covariate if there is a strong positive (or negative) relationship between them: i.e., the lines on the plot should not be horizontal).

b) Is the relationship similar for all treatments? (The lines on the plot should be approximately parallel).

c) Is the covariate influenced by the treatment? (We assume the covariate is not influenced by the treatment and so there should be no separation of the treatment groups along the x-axis on the plot).

These issues are discussed in more detail in Morris (1999).

Analysis description

The data were analysed using a 3-way repeated measures mixed model approach, with Treat 1x and Treat 2x as treatment factors, Day 1x as the repeated factor and Cov1, Cov6 and Cov7 as covariates.This was followed by Planned Comparisons on the predicted means to compare the levels of the effect Treat 1x * Treat 2x * Day 1x.

The compound symmetric covariance structure was used to model the within-subject correlations. When using this structure we assumed that the variability of the responses was the same at each level of Day 1x and the correlation between responses from any pair of levels of Day 1x is the same.

The analysis implements the Kenward-Roger adjustment to the degrees of freedom, Kenward and Roger (1997).

A full description of mixed model theory can be found in Venables and Ripley (2003) and Pinherio and Bates (2002).

For more information on the theoretical approaches that are implemented within this module, see Bate and Clark (2014).
